Overview

The English lexicon refers to the complete set of words used in the English language, including core vocabulary, technical terminology, regional variants, and historical forms.[1]

Estimates of the total number of English words vary depending on criteria such as inclusion of obsolete terms, scientific nomenclature, and loanwords.[2] Modern linguistic studies suggest that English contains over one million distinct lexical items.

Word frequency

Word usage in English follows a highly skewed distribution. A small subset of common words accounts for a large proportion of everyday language, while the majority of words appear infrequently or within specialized domains.[3]
